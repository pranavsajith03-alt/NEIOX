## 4. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

### 4.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website stores on your computer, phone, or tablet when you visit it. They help the site run properly, remember your preferences, and let us understand how the site is being used. Cookies may be:

- **Session cookies** — temporary, and deleted automatically when you close your browser.
- **Persistent cookies** — remain on your device for a set period, or until you delete them.

We may also use similar technologies, such as web beacons (small graphic files that help us understand page views or email opens).

### 4.2 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the Service to function and cannot be switched off in our systems.

- **Hosting & security cookies (Vercel)** — Our Service is hosted on Vercel, which automatically sets certain cookies to maintain security (e.g., bot and abuse protection), manage routing and load balancing, and support core site performance. These cookies do not identify you personally and are not used for advertising.
- **Authentication & session cookies** — If you have a NEIOX account and sign in to the dashboard, we use secure, encrypted session cookies to keep you signed in, verify your identity, and protect your account from unauthorized access.

Because these cookies are strictly necessary, they are set automatically without requiring separate consent. Blocking them will likely prevent login and other core features from working.

### 4.3 Analytics Cookies

We may use analytics tools to understand how visitors use the Service: for example, which pages are viewed, how long visitors stay, and overall traffic and performance trends. This data helps us improve the Service and is generally collected in an aggregated, non-identifying form.

Where any analytics or performance-monitoring tool relies on cookies or similar identifiers, and where required by applicable law, we will only enable them with your consent (e.g., via a cookie banner), and you may withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of any processing carried out before withdrawal.

### 4.4 Managing or Disabling Cookies

Most browsers let you control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

- View and delete stored cookies individually.
- Block cookies from specific sites or all sites.
- Block third-party cookies.
- Clear all cookies automatically when you close your browser.

Cookie settings vary by browser — check your browser's "Help" or "Settings" menu for instructions (commonly available for Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge).

Please note that blocking or deleting essential cookies may prevent parts of the Service — including signing in to your dashboard — from working correctly.
